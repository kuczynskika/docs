# Team collaboration: comments, mentions, and emoji reactions

Collaborate inside MessageDesk without ever sending an unintended text. Use **comments** for internal notes, **mentions** to alert teammates, and **emoji reactions** to respond quickly without typing a message.

---

## Comments

### What are comments?

Comments are **internal-only notes** that appear inside a text thread but **never go to contacts**.\
Use them for:

- Internal handoffs.
- Approvals or QA reviews.
- Context before responding.
- Reminders or documentation.

<Note>
  You're in **comment mode** when the compose box turns **purple** (default theme).
</Note>

<Warning>
  On **mobile**, comments, threaded replies, and mentions are currently **read-only**. Full functionality is coming soon.
</Warning>

---

## Mentions

### What are mentions?

Mentions let you notify teammates directly inside a conversation.

- Type **@** to mention one or more teammates (you can @yourself too).
- Mentions trigger **email** and **browser notifications** that link to the conversation.
- Great for follow-ups, approvals, or assigning the next step.

**Example:**\
"@Jordan please confirm details before closing this ticket."

## Emoji reactions

### What are emoji reactions?

Emoji reactions let you quickly acknowledge or respond to messages and comments without typing a full reply.

- Available on **messages** and **non-threaded comments**.
- Shown in the **top-left** corner of a message or comment.
- Show a **count of unique reactions** (hover or click to see who reacted).

**When to use reactions**

- 👍 Confirm you've seen a message.
- 🎉 Celebrate a win.
- ❤️ Show appreciation.
- 👀 Indicate "reviewing" without replying.

### Inbound reactions from iPhone customers ("Loved", "Liked", etc.)

When a customer reacts to one of your texts from an iPhone (for example, tapping **Loved** on a product photo), Apple sends that reaction over SMS as plain text. You'll see something like `Loved "test outbound"`.

**How it appears in MessageDesk:**

- The reaction lands in the conversation as a normal **inbound message** containing the reaction text and a snippet of the message they reacted to.
- You'll get an inbound message **notification** just like any other reply.
- MessageDesk doesn't render the reaction as a native iOS-style Tapback bubble or attach it visually to the original outbound message. There's no count or icon overlay on the original send.

**Why:** Carrier SMS doesn't carry a structured "reaction" payload between iPhone and non-Apple platforms (Android recipients see the same text strings). MessageDesk receives only the text Apple emits.

<Tip>
  **Tips for iPhone-heavy audiences:**

  - When sending product photos, ask customers to **reply with the product name or number** instead of (or in addition to) tapping a reaction. This keeps intent unambiguous.
  - Use the inbound text itself as the signal. `Loved "Blue floral dress – $48"` clearly maps back to what the customer wants.
</Tip>

## How to use comments, mentions & reactions

### 1. Open a conversation

From your **Inbox**, open any conversation.

### 2. Switch to comment mode

Use the **Message Type dropdown** to switch from **Message** to **Comment**.\
The composer turns **purple**.

<Frame>
  ![Comment Drop Down](/images/comment-drop-down.png)
</Frame>

### 3. Mention teammates

Type **@** anywhere in your comment to open the inline mention menu. You can mention:

- One or multiple teammates.
- Yourself (as a personal reminder).

How the inline mention menu works:

- Type **@** to open the menu, then keep typing to filter by name.
- Use the **arrow keys** to navigate, then press **Enter** to insert the highlighted teammate, or click a name to insert it.
- Teammates appear by their **first \+ last name** (no spaces). If a teammate hasn't set a name, you'll see their **email** instead.

<Note>
  Mentioning yourself is a quick way to set reminders or flag items for follow-up.
</Note>

<Frame>
  ![Comment @ Mention](/images/comment-@-mention.png)
</Frame>

### 4. Write and send your comment

Type your comment, then click **Send**. It appears in the thread but **doesn't** send as a text.

- Optional: click **\+** to [attach media](/messaging/media) (images, files, and so on).
- Switch back to **Message** mode to send an actual SMS.

<Frame>
  ![Published Comment](/images/published-comment.png)
</Frame>

### 5. Add an emoji reaction

Hover over any message or comment, click the **emoji icon**, then choose a reaction.\
Click an existing emoji to see who reacted.

<Frame>
  ![Emoji Reaction](/images/emoji-reaction.png)
</Frame>

### 6. Mentions trigger notifications

Mentioned teammates get:

- An **email notification** with a direct link to the thread.
- A **browser push notification** if enabled.

<Frame>
  ![Mention Notification](/images/mention-notification.png)
</Frame>

2. Open **My Inbox** (left sidebar).
3. Select the pre-saved view for **Comments you're mentioned in**.
4. Click a conversation to jump directly to your mention.

## Searching for comments

Comment search lives in [Advanced Search](/search/advanced-search), not Quick Search. [Quick Search](/search/quick-search) (⌘K) only returns contacts and conversations.

1. Open [Advanced Search](/search/advanced-search):
   - On **desktop**, press **⌘K** / **Ctrl K** to open [Quick Search](/search/quick-search), then press **Enter** on a result or click **Open Advanced Search**.
   - On **mobile or tablet**, tap the search icon near the account settings icon.
2. Type your search query and review the **Comments** results section, which has its own per-type filters and pagination.
3. Click a comment result to open the conversation and scroll directly to that comment.

<Note>
  Search respects your existing permissions. You'll only see comments from conversations you have access to.
</Note>

## Comment threads

### What are comment threads?

Threads let you reply directly to a specific comment or message, keeping related discussions together.

- Organize back-and-forth discussions.
- Keep follow-ups separate from the main message feed.
- Available on desktop (hover) and mobile (force touch).

<Frame>
  ![Comment In Threaad](/images/comment-in-threaad.png)
</Frame>

### Reply in a thread

1. Hover over a comment or message, then click **Reply**.
2. Type your reply.
3. Click **Send**.

<Note>
  You can reply from the **original comment** or the **last message** in a thread. Both add to the same thread.
</Note>

<Frame>
  ![Comment Thread Extended](/images/comment-thread-extended.png)
</Frame>

### When to use threads

- **Follow-ups.** Ask clarifying questions without cluttering the main chat.
- **Status updates.** Keep progress notes in one place.
- **Focused discussions.** Avoid cross-talk in busy conversations.

## Deleting comments

Delete comments you no longer need. Comments are **soft-deleted**. They're removed from view but stay in the database for record-keeping.

### Delete a comment

1. Hover over the comment in the conversation thread.
2. Click the **trash can icon** (🗑️).
3. MessageDesk removes the comment from the thread.

### What happens when you delete a comment

- **Individual comments** (non-threaded): Disappear from the conversation pane entirely.
- **Threaded comments**: A placeholder showing "Comment was deleted" stays to preserve thread context.

<Note>
  Deleted comments are soft-deleted. They no longer appear in the conversation, but they stay in the database and still show up in **conversation exports**.
</Note>

## Tips & best practices

- ✅ **Keep one comment = one ask.** Easier to read and act on.
- 🧭 **Add context before tagging.** Summarize the issue or goal.
- 👥 **Mention the owner.** Tag the responsible teammate so nothing slips through.
- 📎 **Attach files.** Use screenshots, PDFs, or photos for clarity.
- 🔁 **Close the loop.** Add a "Resolved, closing thread" note when finished.
- ⚡ **React instead of reply.** Use emojis for quick acknowledgments to cut noise.

## Privacy & visibility

- Comments are **internal only**. They're never sent to contacts.
- Mentions trigger **email/browser notifications** to the tagged teammates.
- Comments stay in the conversation for **historical context and auditing**.

## FAQ

**Do comments send to the customer?**\
No. They're internal-only.

**How do I know I'm commenting, not texting?**\
The composer turns **purple**, and the message type shows **Comment**.

**Can I mention multiple teammates?**\
Yes. Mention as many as needed.

**Can I attach media to a comment?**\
Yes. Use the **\+** icon to upload files or images.

**Will I get notified if someone mentions me?**\
Yes. Mentions trigger **email** and **browser** notifications.

**How do I reply to a specific comment or message?**\
Hover over it, click **Reply**, and your message appears as a **threaded reply**.

**Can I see all my mentions in one place?**\
Yes. In **My Inbox**, use the **"Comments you're mentioned in"** view.

**Can I delete a comment?**\
Yes. Hover over the comment and click the **trash can icon**. Individual comments disappear entirely. Threaded comments show "Comment was deleted" as a placeholder. Deleted comments are soft-deleted and still appear in conversation exports.

## Troubleshooting

**I don't see the Comment option.**\
Make sure you're inside a specific conversation (not just the list). If it's still missing, check with your admin about permissions.

**Can I search for comments?**\
Yes. Comment search lives in [Advanced Search](/search/advanced-search). On desktop, open [Quick Search](/search/quick-search) with **⌘K** / **Ctrl K** and press **Enter** or click **Open Advanced Search**. On mobile or tablet, tap the search icon near account settings.

**My comment sent as a text.**\
You were probably in **Message** mode. Switch to **Comment** (purple composer) before sending.

**Mentions aren't notifying teammates.**\
Make sure you used **@** to select teammates from the inline menu (arrow keys \+ Enter, or click) instead of just typing their name as plain text.\
Ask teammates to check email spam/junk folders if no notification appears.
